Founder story

RATIOS was born from a moment of urgency, compassion, and responsibility.

What started as one real-life encounter became a mission to help families, caregivers, schools, and first responders protect people who may not be able to communicate clearly during an emergency.

Meet the Founder

Desirae Grissom founded RATIOS after her husband encountered a non-verbal adult with Down syndrome who was wandering alone. That moment exposed a gap that many families know too well: when someone is separated from safety, time matters, communication matters, and identification can change everything.

As a wife, mother, nursing student, advocate, and community leader, Desirae brings compassion, urgency, and lived understanding to this mission. RATIOS exists to make sure vulnerable individuals are not overlooked, misunderstood, or left without support during critical moments.

The organization centers dignity, safety, and community response. It does not define people by diagnosis. It recognizes every person as one of one.

Mission, Vision, Values

Mission

To provide identification resources, safety education, recovery tools, and community awareness for individuals at risk of wandering, elopement, abandonment, or communication barriers during emergencies.

Vision

A community where every person can be identified, supported, and brought home safely with dignity and compassion.

Values

Dignity, safety, urgency, inclusion, caregiver support, first responder collaboration, and practical solutions.
