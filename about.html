Our story

RATIOS was born from compassion, urgency, and a real need.

RATIOS stands for Rescue, Awareness, Tracking, Identification, Outreach, and Safety.

Founder Story

RATIOS was founded by Desirae Grissom after her husband encountered a non-verbal adult with Down syndrome wandering alone. That experience made one thing painfully clear: when a vulnerable person cannot communicate, every second matters.

RATIOS exists to help families, schools, caregivers, first responders, and community members prepare before an emergency happens. The goal is simple but powerful: help every person be recognized, protected, and brought home safely.

Mission

To provide identification resources, caregiver support, recovery tools, community education, and safety awareness for individuals with developmental, cognitive, intellectual, communication, and age-related conditions who may be at risk of wandering or difficulty communicating during emergencies.

Who RATIOS serves

Individuals with Down syndrome

Safety tools and communication supports for those who may need assistance identifying themselves or connecting with caregivers.

Autism & developmental disabilities

Resources for elopement risk, sensory needs, communication preferences, and caregiver emergency planning.

Alzheimer’s disease & dementia

Identification resources and awareness for seniors who may wander or become disoriented.

Fetal alcohol spectrum disorders

Support for individuals who may need safety planning, supervision, and communication tools.

Communication disabilities

Tools for people who are non-verbal, minimally verbal, or unable to communicate clearly during stress.

Families & caregivers

Practical checklists, safety planning, training, and emotional support before and during emergencies.
